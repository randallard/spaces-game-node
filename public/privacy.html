Privacy & Data Sharing

Last updated: 2026

What we collect

When you play Spaces Game, some information about your games may be sent to our server. This is used exclusively for academic research into how artificial intelligence learns to play strategy games.

We never collect your name, email, IP address, cookies, or anything else that could identify you as a real person. This is a hard constraint, not a policy that could change.

Depending on your data sharing choice, we may collect:

  • The board layouts constructed during each round
  • Round outcomes — who won, by how much, whether a trap was triggered
  • An anonymous player ID — a random identifier generated on your device, stored only in your browser's local storage, with no connection to any real identity

How data is used

Collected game data is used to:

  • Train and evaluate reinforcement learning AI models that play Spaces Game
  • Study how human players develop strategies over time
  • Publish academic research (always in aggregate — never per-player)

No data is sold, shared with third parties, or used for advertising.

Your choices

You choose how much to share when you first play, and can change it at any time in your Profile settings.

Full anonymous data

Board and outcome data is collected and linked by your anonymous player ID. This lets us study how individual players improve over time — by game patterns, never by identity. Most useful for research.

Minimal — AI research only

Board and outcome data is collected with no player ID. Each game is treated as independent. Useful for training AI models but cannot track learning across sessions.

No data — offline only

Nothing is sent to our servers for research. Your game data stays entirely on your device. Multiplayer still works — board data in game links is used to run your game but is not stored in our research database.

The anonymous player ID

Your player ID is a randomly generated UUID created the first time you play. It is:

  • Stored in your browser's local storage only
  • Never linked to your name, email, or any account
  • Not sent to the server at all if you choose Minimal or No data
  • Deleted when you clear your browser data or start fresh from a backup restore

If you play on multiple devices, each has its own player ID with no way to link them.

Multiplayer

The server coordinates turn-taking for games with friends by passing board data through game links. This coordination happens regardless of your data sharing choice — it's required for multiplayer to work. With "No data" selected, the board content in those links is processed to run your game but is not written to our research database.

Server backup (coming soon)

We are working on an optional feature that saves your game history and boards to the cloud so you can restore them on a new device. This will be opt-in and separate from research data collection. It will be free initially.

Contact

Questions or requests to delete data associated with your anonymous player ID? Open an issue in the project repository.
